# 12-Week Syllabus  (started: 2026-06-12)

Student priorities: speaking > listening > workplace English.
Assessment note (2026-06-12): production (speaking/writing) currently ~A2+–B1, likely below comprehension.
Plan starts with sentence foundations (articles, past tense) woven into workplace themes, ramps to real-scenario fluency.
~3 lessons/week, 20–30 min each.

## Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Foundations & ear training

### Week 1: Self-introduction & small talk
- Focus skill mix: listening 35% / speaking 40% / reading 15% / writing 10%
- Grammar thread: articles (a/an/the) in everyday phrases — go to THE office, in THE evening
- Scenario: meeting a new colleague; talking about your job and family
- Milestone: give a 5-sentence self-introduction without article mistakes

### Week 2: Storytelling — talking about past experiences
- Focus skill mix: listening 30% / speaking 45% / reading 15% / writing 10%
- Grammar thread: past tense consistency in stories (I had to..., I talked to...)
- Scenario: telling a colleague about a trip or a problem you solved
- Milestone: tell a 6-sentence past-tense story with at most 1 tense slip

### Week 3: Status updates
- Focus skill mix: listening 30% / speaking 40% / reading 15% / writing 15%
- Grammar thread: present perfect vs future (I've finished... / I'll finish it by...)
- Scenario: answering "When can you finish it?" with time + plan + offer (replaces bare "Ten minutes.")
- Milestone: give a 3-part status update: done / doing / next, with timeframes

### Week 4: Asking for clarification
- Focus skill mix: listening 50% / speaking 35% / reading 10% / writing 5%
- Scenario: you didn't catch what someone said — ask again without panic (airport/flight-attendant situations included)
- Milestone: after one hearing of a 1-min audio, ask 2 natural clarification questions and confirm understanding

## Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8): Workplace scenarios

### Week 5: Meetings — joining the discussion
- Focus skill mix: listening 40% / speaking 40% / reading 10% / writing 10%
- Scenario: agreeing, adding a point, asking a question in a team meeting
- Milestone: contribute 3 turns in a simulated 5-min meeting without freezing

### Week 6: Disagreeing politely
- Focus skill mix: listening 30% / speaking 50% / reading 10% / writing 10%
- Scenario: your manager criticizes your plan — respond without over-apologizing or blaming ("I see your point, but..." / "Could we try...")
- Milestone: handle a "boss rejects your proposal" role-play with confident, polite pushback

### Week 7: Email writing
- Focus skill mix: listening 15% / speaking 25% / reading 25% / writing 35%
- Scenario: request, follow-up, and reply emails; tone (not too blunt, not over-sorry)
- Milestone: write a 5-sentence workplace email a native reader finds natural

### Week 8: Summarizing
- Focus skill mix: listening 40% / speaking 30% / reading 15% / writing 15%
- Scenario: summarize a meeting or an audio clip for someone who missed it
- Milestone: summarize a 1-min audio clip in 3 clear sentences

## Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12): Fluency & real-world performance

### Week 9: Presentations
- Focus skill mix: listening 25% / speaking 50% / reading 10% / writing 15%
- Scenario: present a plan in 5 sentences: goal → steps → ask
- Milestone: deliver a structured 1-min presentation script smoothly

### Week 10: Negotiation
- Focus skill mix: listening 30% / speaking 50% / reading 10% / writing 10%
- Scenario: deadlines and workload — push back, propose alternatives, say no politely
- Milestone: negotiate a deadline extension in role-play with 2 alternative offers

### Week 11: Interviews — talking about yourself
- Focus skill mix: listening 25% / speaking 50% / reading 10% / writing 15%
- Scenario: describe achievements and challenges (STAR-style, past tense + results)
- Milestone: answer "tell me about a challenge you handled" in 6+ confident sentences

### Week 12: Debate & course capstone
- Focus skill mix: listening 30% / speaking 50% / reading 10% / writing 10%
- Scenario: defend an opinion under friendly challenge; full review of mastered errors
- Milestone: 3-turn debate holding your position, plus re-do Week 1 self-intro to measure growth
